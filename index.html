About

My research develops tools for analyzing large-scale social data, aiming to provide a better understanding of social structure and behaviors online while also impacting the design of digital social systems. My recent work has focused on reducing political polarization online [ICWSM'22a, ICWSM'22b, WSDM'21, WWW'18], improving the health of online conversations [WWW'21, CSCW'21], and causal inference in social systems [KDD'17 Biometrika'19]. My research often falls at the intersections of Social Networks, Machine Learning, and Causal Inference.
